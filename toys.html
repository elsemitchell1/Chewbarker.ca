Toys

From a young age, German Shepherds are smart, which unfortunately means they’re smart and can be known to be slightly destructive when bored. We recommend providing a variety of stimulating toys for your puppy to keep them entertained and out of trouble. Squeaker toys (animal shapes are encouraged) are always a great option for an energetic and happy pup. They love animal noises and texture in their toys. Lastly, German Shepherds LOVE fetching. Any ball, frisbee, or outside toy will be a hit!

Chewing Toys

Teething toys created for large dogs are great options. You can get hard toys that can be put in the freezer and played with outside for when your dog is teething. Another fan favorite is tug toys. Any large rope toys you and your pup can play with together is always a good choice. Make sure to get a thick rope toys, knots and handles are even better.

Tug Toys

Tug Toys are Chewbarkers favourite and a great training tool. Tug toys can be used on walks to get your dogs attention as well as teaching it new commands. German Shepherds have a high prey drive which means that as soon you start waving a tug toy around they will pay attention.

Fetch Toys

Balls and frisbee's are great for taking to the park and letting your dog chase after them. As the old saying goes a tired dog is a good dog!

About Chewbarker.ca

Chewbarker.ca is a collection of information specifically related to the German Shepherd breed. This site is a useful tool for anyone looking to learn more about this breed of dog or for current owners to stay up-to-date with the breed and connect with other GSD owners. About Me

I’m a second year computer science university student who created this website for my Web Development subject. I decided to create a website about ‘Chewbarker’ who is my (almost) two year old Sable German Shepherd. When researching German Shepherd’s, there was a lot of misinformation about the breed and I wanted a central hub where people could learn about the gsd and about my experience with Chewbarker.
