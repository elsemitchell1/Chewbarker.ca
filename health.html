General Health

Like any dog, especially a large breed dog, German Shepherds have a few major health concerns to be on the lookout for. Due to their large size and high activity level, hip dysplasia is on the top of most lists for the gsd. Another concern to be aware of is Gastric Dilatation-Volvulus (GDV), also called bloat. This occurs when a German Shepherd eats too quickly or straight after exercising.

Growing Pains

Growing pains are inevitable with large breed dogs, however, it’s good to be aware of the signs your pup is in pain and how to treat them. Growing pains or Panosteitis, is a common occurrence in young dogs aged 5-14 months, when there is inflammation on the outer surface or shaft of one or more long bones of the legs. Typically, you’ll see your pup remove pressure from one of their legs and walk on three legs instead of two. Often, even while sitting, they’ll have a paw up off the ground to relieve pressure. It’s best to have your veterinarian take an xray just to be sure your pup has growing pains and they can prescribe medication to reduce inflammation and for pain relief. Often, your pup will have to go without playtime for a week or two to let their body heal.

Hip Displasia

As with most large breed dogs hip displasia is a common problem in German Shepherds. While hip displasia problems can be inherited there are some ways to reduce your dogs risk. Some of these include avoiding jumping and running on hard surfaces especially at a young age, as well as ensuring your dog has a healthy diet and soft bedding.

Early Neutering

While there are some studies showing that early neutering of German Shepherds can be linked to health problems, it is best to consult with your vet to see what they recommend.
