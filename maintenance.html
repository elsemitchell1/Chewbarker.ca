Bathing

German Shepherds should not be bathed too often. This is because excessive bathing can strip their coats of its natural protective oils. We recommend only bathing your German Shepherd once every 3-4 months at most.

Brushing

Let me just say, German Shepherds are known for creating inside tumbleweeds. That being said, there’s plenty an owner can do to minimize the shedding from their dog. Our number one tip is to brush your dog at least twice a week. This will help them excess the old fur and keep their coat manageable. We also recommend investing in a robot vacuum to help save you from vacuuming daily.

Other Maintenance

Other maintenance for your dog including nail trimming and oral care. We recommend trimming your pup's nails every 6-8 weeks to help keep them strong and avoid splitting. For oral care, regular veterinary appointments are a great place to start so your vet can make sure your dog's teeth are in good shape. They may also recommend a tooth brush or cleaning option for your pup to help protect their oral health.

Check-ups

German Shepherd pups need regular health check ups at the vet. Not only is this good for socialising your puppy but it is vital to keep them up to date on their vaccinations as well as making sure there are no underlying medical issues.
