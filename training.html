Training

The biggest thing you can do to help you pup’s training is to be consistent and use a routine. My pup knows when it's 5pm and he is ready to go out the door for his daily walk and exercising. We recommend daily exercise for this breed. Be mindful to include stimulation in your training. Using toys and rewards is encouraged. We also recommend trying out new games with your dog and always celebrate their successes when completing commands. Some basic commands to try out include: sit, stay, wait, leave it, down, up, and heel. For more information on training, we recommend asking your local vet for a reward-based trainer, information on local dog parks, and any local dog groups.

What to expect

German Shepherds are smart dogs and often find ways to train you. To avoid this type of situation you should have a structured training plan laid out before even bringing your GSD home with you. Avoid letting your GSD dictate when you feed or walk it as they will often whine to make you do what they want. The best course of action is to ignore this behaviour and stick to a structured routine.

Crate Training

Crate Training is a great way to keep your dog and your home safe while working or sleeping. It is also a great tool when you have a young puppy as a correctly used crate will stop them making mistakes inside the house. Another great reason for crate training is that a crate is often treated like a den for them and creates a safe space for your dog.
