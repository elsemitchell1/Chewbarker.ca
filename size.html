Sizes

German Shepherds tend to grow very quickly from birth. A full grown male will generally be between 80 - 90lbs and a full grown female will generally be between 65 - 70lbs. However, there are many cases of fully grown German Shepherds being well over 100lbs. Below will give you an idea of their growth stages and shows Chewbarker growing up.

2 months

This is Chewbarker at 2 months old just after we brought him home. He was around 20lbs as he is slightly larger than the standard for German Shepherds.

4 months

This is Chewbarker at 4 months old and about 35 - 40lbs. Don't let his cute appearance fool you, at this age German Shepherds are easily compared to sharks and velociraptors.

6 months

At this age Chewbarker was around 50 - 60lbs. Note the floppy ears, this happens with young German Shepherds as they are growing and it is completely normal to have ears that are standing up one day and flopping around the next.

6 - 12 months

In this photo Chewbarker is around 10 - 11 months of age and weighing about 85 - 90lbs. Around this age is when you will see your German Shepherd begin to calm down slightly. Although this can take more or less time depending on the dogs temperament.

2 years

Chewbarker is about a year and a half old in this photo. He is a little over 100lbs and we expect him to gain a few more pounds still as German Shepherds aren't expected to be fully grown until about 2.5 years old.

About Chewbarker.ca

Chewbarker.ca is a collection of information specifically related to the German Shepherd breed. This site is a useful tool for anyone looking to learn more about this breed of dog or for current owners to stay up-to-date with the breed and connect with other GSD owners. Thank you for visiting my website and please add a pic of your pup on the gallery page!

About Me

I’m a second year computer science university student who created this website for my Web Development subject. I decided to create a website about ‘Chewbarker’ who is my (almost) two year old Sable German Shepherd. When researching German Shepherd’s, there was a lot of misinformation about the breed and I wanted a central hub where people could learn about the gsd and about my experience with Chewbarker.
